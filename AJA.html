Protein pancakes

These pancakes are gluten free, vegan and guilt free!!

Made with simple ingredients you can find around your kitchen for when you have a sweet craving in a morning... because I know I defiantly do!! They make the perfect breakfast with plenty protein and carbohydrates to fuel you for the morning.

You will need:

  • Protein powder of your choice (I use MyProtein Vanilla!)
  • 1 ripe banana
  • 50g of gluten free oats
  • 1/2 tbsp baking powder
  • Milk (of any kind)
  • Cinnamon (optional)
  • 1/2 tbsp coconut oil or spray oil
  • Toppings

How to make it:

  1. Mash up the banana in a bowl
  2. Blend up oats until they become powder
  3. Mix together all the dry ingredients until combined
  4. Add the banana and mix together well
  5. Slowly add milk to the mixture until a parable thick batter is formed
  6. Heat up a pan with oil and wait until hot
  7. Pour part of the mixture into pan to form a pancake
  8. Flip pancake to cook both sides as desired
  9. Once all the mixture has been used serve on a plate with desired toppings

Topping Ideas:

  • Fruit - Blueberries, Strawberries, Cranberries
  • Maple syrup/Golden syrup
  • Melted chocolate spread
  • Lemon juice and sugar

Chickpea Cookies

A natrually gluten-free, vegan and a great alternatice to regular cookies

Cookies

Sounds very strange but these chickpea cookies are really delicious. You can make them by using ingredients that you could probably find in your cupboard at home and making it really easy!

Truth is I was quite sceptical at first when I thought about the idea of putting chickpeas in a cookie but honestly I'd be lying if I said I didn't enjoy them. They're loaded with chocolate chips and peanut butter so that's mostly what you taste... what could you hate about that flavour combination?

You will need:

  • 1 can chickpeas
  • 1 cup oats
  • 1/3 cup peanut butter (adjust to preference but no less than)
  • 1/3 cup agave/maple syrup or honey
  • 1 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 cup chocolate chips

How to make:

  1. Drain and wash chickpeas and place in food processor alongside peanut butter, vanilla extract, baking powder, maple syrup and oats
  2. Blend ingredients until smooth
  3. Fold chocolate chips into the mixture
  4. Drop batter by tablespoon onto a lined baking tray and bake for around 12-14 mins at 180 degrees celsius

Millionaire Shortbread

I've seen and tried so many gluten free millionaire shortbread recipes out there and I haven't really found one I love... so after a number of attempts at making my own, I have finally tweaked my recipe to the point were it tastes SUPER GOOD!! This recipe is gluten free and can easily made vegan with the chocolate and butter being switched for alternatives.

You will need:

  • 250g gluten free flower
  • 150g chocolate
  • 150g butter
  • 75g caster sugar
  • 100g desiccated coconut (adjust to preference)
  • 12 madjool dates
  • 30g maple syrup
  • 1 tbsp coconut oil
  • 1 tbsp coconut sugar

How to make it:

  1. Line a 8/10" baking tray with grease proof paper
  2. Pre haet oven to 180 degrees
  3. Place butter and caster sugar into bowl and mix until fluffy
  4. Mix flour in a well until a dough forms
  5. Press dough into the tray and bake for 20 mins or until golden brown
  6. Soak dates in hot water 10 mins
  7. Blend dates with a small amount of water until a paste is formed
  8. Add maple syrup, desiccated coconut and coconut sugar to paste and mix well
  9. Once the base is cool spread paste onto and freeze for 30 mins
  10. melt chocolate and coconut oil and spread onto the base. Then freeze for a further 20 mins

Top Tip: Keep in a cool environment or fridge after cut to preserve at best!
